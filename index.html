Kongregate Games

These flash games were released and can surprisingly still be played on the web at kongregate.com/accounts/DaFlamingPotato. They use the same flash player emulator (ruffle) that I am using to display the games here on this page. For a while none of the games worked, but since ruffle was released they are playable again! If you remember any favorite flash games from your childhood, chances are you can play them again thanks to ruffle.

Click the links below to play the released version on Kongregate. Only downside is there are ads, so I added an embedded version here if you don't want to deal with that.

You can read (in quotations) the original game descriptions that I wrote at the time as well. Enjoy!

2009

The Quiz (DEMO) v1.1 -- This was the first game that I ever made. Heavily inspired by the Impossible Quiz games. I read a book that I think I borrowed from my friend Bobby about Flash games. Downloaded Macromedia Flash 8 and the rest is history. I still remember that I really enjoyed making this game. There is something magical about making your own thing from scratch--seeing it come alive on screen, and being able to interact with it.

"A simple quiz game, point and click."

Basic Snake Game -- Not much to say about this one, other than I remember playing Snake all the time on my old Nokia brick phone after tennis practice. Pretty sure I ripped this game straight out of a tutorial... but no one needs to know that. Except now you do.

"This is a basic snake game, if you have any questions on how to do this it is very easy, one of the easiest games out there. Eat the food to grow, and don’t hit yourself! You CAN go through walls."

Basic Snake Game 2 -- Again not much to say, other than I appeared to have some fixation with snake games. This game may or may not have also been ripped from a tutorial. But I added my own stuff this time, too! Progress people, progress.

"This is the awesome sequel to basic snake game, featuring a completely new interface with freeplay and level select modes. You can even play seizure madness mode, same as hard mode buut with fun flashy colors! :D So step right up and try your hand at this classic addicting game, updated with cool new options for you convienience!"

Knil's Flash Adventure -- Unfortunately Knil's Flash Adventure does not work currently. Something to due with the fact it was made in Stencyl? Not sure. I'm hoping to find a solution, because it was one of the more complete games that I made.

2012

Leather Pants -- Inspiration from LEATHER PANTS~! -- by Little Kuriboh -- https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0jnCcejkpjg -- No I did not get permission to use the song. Please don't sue me.

"John lost his leather pants. Help him take it off his mind. No, this isn’t a serious game. Just for teh lolz."

Fun facts:
Pressing "s" does a kick animation. Pressing "s" while jumping just makes you faceplant.
Pressing "d" while jumping does a flying backflip / kick.

DaFlaming Maze Demo -- "Another crappy maze game. It’s ridiculously easy to cheat still, but since it’s only a demo I don’t care. See if you can beat it without cheating!"

Fun facts:
Right-click was a common trick to get around walls in maze games, which is why it was disabled.
However you can still cheat by holding down left click while you move the mouse to go through walls.
The key level is the only one that you can't skip completely, because you have to activate a cutscene and collect all the keys to beat the level.

Platform Game (Site Unlocked)

Beta version of my first real platformer game, besides Leather Pants. I had to decompile the .swf and remove the site blocker code to get it to run. Which was apparently super easy to do. So even though I tried to keep people from ripping my games, here I am ripping my own game, years later... Sorry past me.

Fun facts:
It is possible to jump over the wall using the spring.
Holding down arrow shows a crouching sprite.
Pressing "S" or "D" just makes the character disappear. Probably some other animation/action that I never finished. Possibly leftover from "Leather Pants".
The character assets are borrowed from the flash game "Shift".
